Conjunction Fallacy


Violation of the conjunction rule: the probability of the conjunction of two events, P(A&B) cannot exceed the probability of its constituents, P(A) and P(B)1

Tversky and Kahneman (1983)1

Study 1

2020 NFL Superbowl between the Kansas City Chiefs and the San Francisco 49ers

For study 1...

Participants



361 participants recruited from CloudResearch
  • Friday and Saturday before Superbowl
  • KC expected to win by 1.5 points
  • Targeted Football fans in California, Kansas, Iowa, and Missouri
  • 43.8% female, Mean Age = 44.1


Study Design


Participants received an adapted version of CF from Tversky and Kahneman (1983)
  • The Kansas City Chiefs will win the Superbowl against the San Francisco 49ers
  • The Kansas City Chiefs will have a lower score than the San Francisco 49ers at halftime
  • The Kansas City Chiefs will have a lower score than the San Francisco 49ers at halftime but will win the Superbowl
  • The Kansas City Chiefs will have a higher score than the San Francisco 49ers at halftime but will lose the Superbowl

Study 2

2021 NBA Play-In Tournament between the Los Angeles Lakers and Golden State Warriors

For study 2...

Participants


325 participants recruited from Prolific
  • Several days leading up to play-in game
  • Lakers expected to win by 3.5 points
  • Targeted basketball fans in California
  • 26% female, Mean Age = 34.3


Study Design


Participants read 8 pairs of statements, each with a conjunction and one of its constituents
  • The Warriors will have more turnovers in the the game than the Lakers
  • The Warriors will have more turnovers in the the game but will win the game against the Lakers


4 Pro-Lakers and 4 Pro-Warriors conjunction statements (within-subjects)





Study 3

2021 Euro Match between Scotland and England National Teams

For study 3...

Participants


453 participants recruited from Prolific
  • Several days leading up to EURO match
  • England expected to win by 1.5 goals
  • Targeted soccer fans in England, Scotland, and New Zealand
  • 61.9% female, Mean Age = 35.8


Study Design


Participants read 12 pairs of statements, each with a conjunction and one of its constituents
  • England will score fewer goals than Scotland before halftime
  • England will score fewer goals before halftime but go on to win the match against Scotland


4 Pro-England, 4 Pro-Scotland, and 4 neutral conjunction statements (within-subjects)
