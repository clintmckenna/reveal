What is Approach-Avoidance motivation?

Approach motivation is the energization of behavior by, or the direction of behavior toward, positive stimuli...avoidance motivation is the energization of behavior by, or the direction of behavior away from, negative stimuli

Study 1

Concept Activation
For study 1, we are interested in whether ppl have a distinct concept for app/avd and action/inaction that they can access

Study 1

  • 46 Mturk participants
  • Participants rated words on approach/avoidance and action/inaction

Study 1 Predictions

People will have distinct concepts word ratings, such that:
  • approach/avoidance words will differ on action ratings
  • action/inaction words will differ on approach ratings

Approach Ratings Instructions

Does this word refer to approaching a positive stimulus ("approach"), or does this word refer to avoiding a negative stimulus ("avoid")?

Example of approaching a positive stimulus: A mouse approaching a piece of cheese
Example of avoiding a negative stimulus: A mouse avoiding a cat

Action Ratings Instructions

Does this word refer to engaging in mental or physical action ("action"), or does this word refer to a lack of mental or physical action ("inaction")?

Example of action: running or concentrating on a difficult problem
Example of inaction: standing still or not using mental effort

Action categories: t(45) = 8.85, p < .001

Inaction categories: t(45) = 8.02, p < .001

Approach categories: t(45) = 7.12, p < .001

Avoid categories: t(45) = 6.09, p < .001

Study 1 Summary

  • People can report differences in approach/avoidance and action/inaction dimensions
  • Stimuli usable for priming concepts
  • People struggle with distinction between action and approach interactions




Study 2

Cognitive Reflection

Cognitive Reflection Test

  • Measures tendency to override intuitive response
  • Correlated with many measures of biases, intelligence, and thinking styles

Frederick (2005)

A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost? _____ cents

  • Intuitive: 10 cents
  • Correct: 5 cents


Frederick (2005)

Priming Induction

Participants completed word stems

  • E S C _ P E
  • A D V _ N C E
  • P _ U S E
  • O B S E R _ E

Study 2 Predictions

Action and Approach motivations will have interacting effects, such that:
  • action/approach words will facilitate quicker access to intuition, resulting in lower accuracy
  • action/inaction words will facilitate slower access to intuition, resulting in higher accuracy

Participants

  • 199 Mturk participants
  • Mean age 35.22
  • 45.7% female

Study 3

Mouse Tracking

Mouse-tracking Paradigm

  • 10 CRT items
  • Competition between intuitive and correct response
  • Emphasis on process
Freeman and Ambady (2010)
Duran et al. (2017)

Study 3 Predictions

Competing hypotheses:
  • If action/approach facilitates quicker access to intuition, we will see less competition (lower AUC)
  • If action/approach facilitates quicker access to intuition, which prompts an override we will see more competition (higher AUC)

Participants

  • 140 UM undergrads
  • Mean age 18.95
  • 57.1% female

Prepreprocessing

  • Select only correct trials
  • Align trajectories to same side
  • Time and Spatial standardization
  • Remove +/- 3 SD AUC
  • Converted to z-scores for readability
Freeman and Ambady (2010)
Kieslich et al. (2019)

Issues

  • Small sample size
  • Limited number of trials
  • Noisy



Conclusion

Conclusion

  • Individuals can report distinct concepts for approach/avoidance and action/inaction
  • Performance on CRT best under inaction/avoid
  • Action/approach activation may be prompting an override of intuition, leading to extra effort
Freeman and Ambady (2010)
Kieslich et al. (2019)
