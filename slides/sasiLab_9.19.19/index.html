A Process Investigation of Expressive Rationality

Clint McKenna
September 19th, 2019

Expressive Rationality

The rational way to use one’s intellect is to process information in a way that promotes consistent belief formation (Kahan et al., 2017)

“...the antagonism between identity-protective cognition and perceptual accuracy is not a consequence of too little rationality but too much” (Kahan, 2017)

Issues

    People may be arriving at the correct answer by...
  • Using the wrong strategy
  • Guessing
  • Overt expressive responding

Participants

  • 932 self-identified Democrats and Republicans on MTurk
  • 54.3% female

    Exclusions
  • 20 failed data quality check
  • 22 reported or logged technical errors
  • 26 had no transitions between cells

Procedure

Same procedure as Kahan, but obscured cells unless participants hovered over area with mouse cursor

Gun Control Beliefs

    Items adapted from Pew American Trends Panel, loaded on single factor (α = .92)

    Example Items
  • “I think gun violence is a big problem in the United States"
  • "Overall, I think gun laws should be a lot more strict than they are today”

    Scale was coded such that higher values = more "friendly" conclusion for a given condition

Numeracy

    Measured test of statistical competence using 4-item Berlin Numeracy Test (Cokely et al., 2012)

    Example Item
    Out of 1,000 people in a small town 500 are members of a choir. Out of these 500 members in the choir 100 are men. Out of the 500 inhabitants that are not in the choir 300 are men. What is the probability that a randomly drawn man is a member of the choir? Please indicate the probability in percent:
  • 10%
  • 25%
  • 40%
  • None of the above

Dependent Variables

  • Was the study interpretation correct?
  • Was the strategy for solving the problem correct?
  • How many mouse transitions between cells were there?
  • How long did participants fixate on "Cell A"?

    Any stats report coefficients from full model (including 3-way interaction between gun control vs. rash condition, friendly belief score, and numeracy)

What about Numeracy?

Numeracy did not predict correct interpretation, total transitions, or Cell A fixation
...Trend towards correct interpretation, B = 1.52, p = .09

Did predict correct strategy, OR = 2.24, p = .002

Conclusion


Results cast doubt on suggested processes for motivated numeracy

People may be right for the wrong reasons (or don't have a good reason)

Ss may be limited by cognitive load and the study design being "one-shot"
